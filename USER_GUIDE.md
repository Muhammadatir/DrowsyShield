# DrowsyGuard User Guide

## Welcome to DrowsyGuard

DrowsyGuard is your intelligent companion for safer driving. Using advanced facial detection technology, it monitors your alertness in real-time and helps prevent drowsy driving accidents.

## Getting Started

### Installation

#### Mobile Devices (Recommended)
1. Open DrowsyGuard in your mobile browser (Chrome, Safari)
2. Look for the "Install" button or banner
3. Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install"
4. Launch DrowsyGuard from your home screen

#### Desktop Browsers
1. Visit DrowsyGuard in Chrome, Edge, or supported browser
2. Click the install icon in the address bar
3. Select "Install" when prompted
4. Access DrowsyGuard from your apps menu

### First-Time Setup

#### 1. Create Your Account
- Enter your email address
- Create a secure password
- Verify your email (check spam folder if needed)
- Sign in to your account

#### 2. Camera Permission
- Grant camera access when prompted
- This is essential for drowsiness detection
- Your camera data stays on your device - never transmitted

#### 3. Calibrate the System
- Follow the on-screen calibration wizard
- Position your face in the guide frame
- Blink naturally when prompted
- Test the alert to ensure it works
- Save your calibration settings

## Using DrowsyGuard

### Starting a Monitoring Session

1. **Position Your Device**
   - Mount your phone on the dashboard
   - Ensure camera has clear view of your face
   - Adjust brightness for visibility

2. **Start Monitoring**
   - Tap the large "Start Monitoring" button
   - Keep your eyes on the road
   - The app monitors you in the background

3. **During Monitoring**
   - Small camera preview shows in corner
   - Real-time graph displays your alertness
   - Incident counter tracks drowsiness events
   - Session timer shows elapsed time

### Understanding Alerts

#### Alert Levels
- **Green**: Fully alert and attentive
- **Yellow**: Mild drowsiness detected
- **Red**: Dangerous drowsiness - take action!

#### When Alert Triggers
- Loud audio alarm sounds
- Phone vibrates strongly
- Visual warning displays
- Tap "Acknowledge" to confirm you're awake

#### What to Do
1. Pull over safely when possible
2. Take a break - walk around
3. Get some fresh air
4. Have a caffeinated beverage
5. Consider stopping for a nap

### Adjusting Settings

#### Sensitivity
- **High**: More sensitive, alerts sooner
- **Medium**: Balanced detection
- **Low**: Alerts only for severe drowsiness

#### Alert Preferences
- **Volume**: Adjust alert sound level
- **Vibration**: Set vibration intensity
- **Auto-start**: Enable automatic monitoring

#### Camera Settings
- **Front/Rear**: Choose camera
- **Resolution**: Adjust for performance
- **Brightness**: Compensate for lighting

### Viewing Your History

#### Session List
- View all past driving sessions
- Sort by date, duration, or incidents
- See summary statistics

#### Session Details
- Drowsiness incident timeline
- Alertness level graph
- Session duration and metrics
- Export data for records

### Emergency Features

#### Emergency Contacts
1. Go to Settings > Emergency Contacts
2. Add trusted contacts (name, phone)
3. Contacts saved securely
4. Quick dial during emergencies

#### Emergency Mode
- Access from monitoring screen
- One-tap emergency call
- Shares your location automatically
- Sends alert to emergency contacts

## Privacy & Security

### Your Data is Private
- **Local Processing**: All facial detection happens on your device
- **No Video Upload**: Camera feed never leaves your phone
- **Encrypted Storage**: Your data is encrypted and secure
- **User Control**: Delete your data anytime

### What We Store
- Session statistics (duration, incidents)
- User preferences and settings
- Calibration data
- Emergency contact information

### What We Don't Store
- Camera images or video
- Your exact location (only used in emergencies)
- Any identifiable facial data

## Troubleshooting

### Camera Not Working
- Check camera permissions in phone settings
- Ensure no other app is using camera
- Restart DrowsyGuard
- Try switching to other camera

### Alerts Not Triggering
- Recalibrate the system
- Check face is visible in preview
- Improve lighting conditions
- Adjust sensitivity settings
- Ensure phone isn't in power-saving mode

### App Not Responding
- Close and restart the app
- Clear browser cache
- Check for app updates
- Ensure stable internet for sync

### Offline Functionality
- Core monitoring works offline
- Data syncs when connection returns
- History available offline
- Settings changes save locally

## Battery Management

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is DrowsyGuard 100% accurate?
No system is perfect. DrowsyGuard is a safety aid, not a replacement for adequate rest. If you're tired, the safest option is always to rest before driving.

### Can I use it at night?
Yes! The system works in various lighting conditions. However, ensure there's enough light on your face for detection.

### Does it work offline?
Core monitoring features work offline. Session data syncs when you're back online.

### How much battery does it use?
Typical usage: 10-15% battery per hour. Actual usage varies by device and settings.

### Can multiple people use one account?
Each person should calibrate separately for best results. Consider individual accounts for accurate tracking.

### Is my data shared with anyone?
No. Your data is private and never shared with third parties. All processing is local to your device.
